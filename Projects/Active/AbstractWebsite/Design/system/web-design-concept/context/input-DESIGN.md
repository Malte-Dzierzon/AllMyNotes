# # Design Concept – Design System

## Overview

# Design Concept – Noise / Editorial / Retro-Futuristic Web

## Vision

The website should intentionally move away from modern minimalist interfaces. Instead of focusing on perfection, the design should prioritize atmosphere and emotion.

The visual direction should feel like a combination of experimental graphic design, digital magazines, vintage CRT monitors, and a calm, melancholic vision of the future.

This is not classic cyberpunk with neon rain, excessive HUD elements, and visual overload. Instead, it is a reduced, colder, and more emotional interpretation of a futuristic aesthetic.

---

## Mood

The overall atmosphere should feel:

- calm
- melancholic
- futuristic
- experimental
- editorial
- slightly surreal
- like a digital dream
- inspired by nostalgic technology
- focused on high-quality graphic design

The website should feel more like an artwork or digital exhibition than a traditional interface.

---

## Color System

### Primary Colors

- cold white
- blue-tinted gray
- deep navy
- black

### Accent Colors

- desaturated cyan
- cold neon blue
- subtle silver tones
- very restrained glow effects

Avoid bright neon colors and strong RGB aesthetics.

The palette should feel cold, cinematic, and refined.

---

## Visual Language

The design should be driven by textures rather than colors.

Key visual elements:

- film grain / noise
- CRT scanlines
- dithering
- halftone patterns
- subtle blur effects
- bloom
- light leaks
- analog artifacts
- JPEG and video compression effects
- soft vignette

Everything should have a slightly imperfect, organic, and aged digital feeling.

---

## Typography

The typography should feel editorial rather than like a traditional UI system.

Principles:

- large expressive headlines
- asymmetric compositions
- generous whitespace
- Japanese typography used as a visual design element
- small technical labels and metadata
- combination of sans-serif and monospace fonts

Text is not only information — it is part of the visual composition.

---

## Layout

The layout should not strictly follow conventional UI rules.

Characteristics:

- free-form composition
- layered and overlapping elements
- blocks with different scales
- magazine-inspired structure
- poster-like aesthetics
- dynamic grid systems

Each section should have the feeling of its own cover page, poster, or editorial spread.

---

## Animation

Animations should be slow, subtle, and atmospheric.

Examples:

- slowly moving noise textures
- floating elements
- CRT flickering effects
- soft blur transitions
- slow parallax movement
- gentle fade animations

Avoid fast, distracting, or overly playful motion.

The movement should feel cinematic and calm.

---

## Audio

Optional background music.

Direction:

- ambient
- dreamcore
- experimental electronic
- atmospheric soundscapes
- space ambient
- calm synthesizer layers

The audio should enhance the atmosphere and always be easily disabled by the user.

---

## Overall Impression

The website should feel like:

> A digital art exhibition, an experimental album cover, and a futuristic magazine combined — calm, atmospheric, mysterious, and emotionally engaging.

Not loud.

Not playful.

Not typical cyberpunk.

Instead:

A reduced, textured, cinematic, and emotional digital experience.
